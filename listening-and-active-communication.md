# **What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening?**

* Maintain eye contact
* Be fully present
* Don’t interrupt
* Use verbal and non-verbal encouragement
* Ask open-ended questions
* Avoid judging or giving solutions too early
* Summarize or paraphrase when needed
* Show empathy through tone and body language

---

# **According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?**

* Slow down the conversation
* Understand both the message and the emotions of the speaker
* Listen for content
* Listen for feelings
* Reflect back in your own words
* Validate the speaker’s experience
* Avoid judgment, advice, or premature solutions
* Focus on the speaker, not on your own response
* Check for accuracy (“Did I get that right?”)

**Essence →** Understand first, don’t assume, reflect back without judgment.

---

# **What are the obstacles in your listening process?**

* Thinking about my response while the other person is still speaking
* Missing details because I focus on what I want to say next
* Forming judgments too quickly when I disagree
* Evaluating the speaker internally instead of listening openly
* Getting distracted by external factors like noise or notifications
* Losing focus due to wandering thoughts

---

# **What can you do to improve your listening?**

* Be fully present in conversations
* Avoid multitasking and remove distractions like phone or tabs
* Practice patience and allow the speaker to finish
* Avoid jumping to advice or solutions too quickly
* Reflect back what the speaker said
* Ask clarifying questions to ensure understanding
* Listen completely before responding

---

# **When do you switch to a Passive communication style?**

* When I feel uncomfortable expressing my needs
* When I worry that my opinion may cause conflict
* Around dominant or outspoken people
* When someone speaks forcefully, and I hold back my thoughts
* When I prioritize others’ feelings over my own
* When I agree to plans I don’t prefer just to maintain harmony

---

# **When do you switch into Aggressive communication styles?**

* When I feel ignored or interrupted repeatedly
* When I feel unheard and try to force my point
* When frustration or impatience takes over
* When I’m stressed or overwhelmed
* When someone asks for something unexpectedly during busy moments
* When my tone becomes sharp or irritated unintentionally

---

# **When do you switch into Passive-Aggressive communication styles?**

* When I feel upset but don’t express my feelings directly
* When I withdraw, give short responses, or use silent treatment
* When I avoid discussing issues even though they bother me
* When my efforts go unnoticed or unappreciated
* When I respond with sarcasm to show dissatisfaction indirectly
* When I expect people to understand my feelings without me stating them

---

# **How can you make your communication assertive?**

* Be clear about your needs and feelings
* Take a moment to understand what you want to express
* Use “I” statements to avoid blaming
* Communicate experiences like “I feel…” or “I need…”
* Maintain a calm and respectful tone
* Use confident but non-threatening body language
* Reflect on past interactions to learn and improve
* Practice assertive responses in simple, everyday situations
* Be confident in expressing your views
* Stay open to feedback and others’ perspectives
* Set healthy boundaries when needed
* Say “no” respectfully when something is not right for you
